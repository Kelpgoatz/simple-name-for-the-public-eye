Melbourne could be called Batman.

By Staff Writer - June 21 - 4 min read

It's a common joke on social media that Melbourne is Gotham City. The joke goes deeper than people think: the man who first found and controversially bought the land now known as Melbourne was named John Batman.

Not quite like the hero you know, John Batman left behind a name that still gets people doing a double take whenever Melbourne and Gotham get mentioned in the same breath.

Batman's Treaty is where the story gets even stranger. Although the deal had already been done, it was never truly recognised by the British Empire, and most people looking at what was traded can understand why it was dismissed as an "unmitigated sham."

The deal, while introducing some unfamiliar items that may have been useful in daily life, was widely seen as being nowhere near worth the whole of Melbourne.

"Unmitigated sham."

That reputation is part of why the joke survives online today. The name Batman sounds theatrical, the city already feels dramatic, and the historical footnote behind it makes the whole thing feel even more Gotham-like.

However people frame it, the connection between Melbourne and Batman is one of those odd little historical details that keeps turning into a perfect internet joke.
